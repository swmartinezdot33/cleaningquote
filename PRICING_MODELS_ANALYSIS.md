**Business Profile**:
- Solo to small team
- Primarily one service area
- Basic CRM or spreadsheet-based tracking
- Looking to professionalize

**Expected Impact**:
- Saves 20-25 hours/week
- Increases quote conversion 25%
- Additional $15,000-30,000/year revenue
- Reduces administrative overhead

**Value Proposition**:
- "Automate your quote process, focus on service"
- "Get paid for selling, not paperwork"
- "Professional quoting = more sales"

**Justification at $80,000 (midpoint)**:

### Value-Based Pricing Strategy

**Determining the Right Price**:

1. **Analyze Customer Business Size**
   - Number of employees
   - Annual revenue
   - Growth trajectory
   - Operational complexity

2. **Calculate Expected ROI**
   - Current staff costs (salary × hours saved)
   - Expected revenue increase
   - Operational efficiency gains
   - Strategic value

3. **Price Accordingly**
   - Small: 15-20% of first-year benefit
   - Mid-market: 20-25% of first-year benefit
   - Enterprise: 25-30% of first-year benefit

4. **Justify with Numbers**
   - Show labor savings
   - Estimate revenue increase
   - Calculate break-even timeline
   - Demonstrate 3-year ROI

### Value-Based Model Advantages

✅ **Maximum Revenue**: Price tied to customer value
✅ **Customer Satisfaction**: Price matches value received
✅ **Premium Positioning**: Show business impact
✅ **Upsell Potential**: Future growth = higher value
✅ **Justifiable**: Clear ROI calculation
✅ **Competitive**: No direct competitor comparison

### Value-Based Model Disadvantages

❌ **Sales Complexity**: More consultative selling
❌ **Time to Close**: Requires more discovery
❌ **Negotiation**: Customer may push back on price
❌ **Accuracy Risk**: ROI estimates may be wrong
❌ **Pricing Consistency**: Hard to maintain across customers
❌ **Team Training**: Sales team needs ROI training

### When to Use Value-Based Model

- Strong relationship with customer
- Customer has clear business goals
- Can accurately quantify impact
- Premium market positioning desired
- Customer profitability varies widely

## Hybrid Models

### Combination Approaches

Some organizations use combinations of the above models:

---

### Model A: Fixed + Tiered

**Structure**:
- Fixed price for 80% of customers ($115,000)
- Tiered options for price-sensitive customers ($65K-95K)
- Higher tiers for enterprise ($125K+)

**Strategy**:
- Lead with fixed price as "full platform"
- Offer tiered if customer pushes back
- Suggest professional tier as "best value"

---

### Model B: Tiered + Value-Based

**Structure**:
- Tier-based feature sets
- Tier pricing adjusted based on value analysis
- Small: $65K base, up to $75K for high-value
- Mid: $95K base, up to $110K for strategic value
- Enterprise: $125K base, up to $150K for complex needs

**Strategy**:
- Start with tier recommendation
- Adjust pricing based on calculated ROI
- Transparent pricing with clear justification

---

### Model C: Value-Based Primary + Tiers Fallback

**Structure**:
- Primary: Value-based pricing ($75K-135K range)
- Fallback: Standard tiers if customer won't engage on ROI
- Flexible: Can mix features across tiers if needed

**Strategy**:
- Most consultative approach
- Takes more time but maximizes revenue
- Best for enterprise sales

## Model Recommendation

### Primary Recommendation: FIXED PRICE ($115,000)

**Reasoning**:

1. **Market Position**: Premium, enterprise-grade
2. **Sales Efficiency**: Simplest to present and close
3. **Revenue Optimization**: No discounting pressure
4. **Consistency**: Same offering to all customers
5. **Margin Clarity**: Straightforward profit calculation
6. **Negotiation Simplicity**: One number to defend

**Implementation**: Lead with fixed price in all initial presentations.

### Secondary Recommendation: TIERED MODEL for flexibility

If fixed price creates sales resistance, fall back to tiered model:
- Starter: $65,000 (for price-sensitive buyers)
- Professional: $95,000 (recommended tier)
- Enterprise: $125,000 (for large customers)

**Use When**:
- Customer objects to price
- Market segment requires options
- Want to expand addressable market

### Tertiary Recommendation: VALUE-BASED for strategic customers

For large enterprise deals or custom situations:
- Analyze customer business impact
- Calculate ROI
- Price at 20-30% of first-year value
- Range: $75,000-$135,000

**Use When**:
- Customer interested in ROI justification
- Complex organizational buying
- Long sales cycle
- High-value strategic deal
